Career

He made his acting debut at a young age, in a 2004 episode of the long-running Polish soap opera Plebania. He was later cast as a series regular in Ojciec Mateusz (2008-2011), but his big break came when he was cast as a teen idol in Rodzinka.pl (2011-2020). As his popularity grew, he began co-hosting The Voice of Poland and starred in the feature film My Own Pole, both in 2013. He studied Philosophy at the University of Warsaw from 2014 to 2015. In 2016, he received the Telekamery award for his work in television. He served as an ambassador for World Youth Day 2016, which was held in Kraków.

Musia gained international acclaim for his role in the 2018 Netflix series 1983, which he also executive produced. In 2019, he was named to Forbes’ 30 Under 30 Europe list in the Entertainment category for his work. He appeared in the 2019 Netflix fantasy drama series The Witcher as well as the 2021 Canal+ Premium series Klangor. In 2021, he received a master’s degree in acting from the AST National Academy of Theatre Arts in Kraków.
